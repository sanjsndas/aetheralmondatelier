    📌 What Are Cookies? (Alchemy Edition)

    Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help remember your preferences, enhance functionality, and sometimes – but not on our site – track behavior. At Aether Almond, we use only strictly necessary cookies when required, plus minimal session identifiers for contact form coherence. We never deploy advertising or analytics cookies that follow you beyond our crimson kitchen.

    ✨ Our golden rule: Your browsing is your own. No third-party scripts, no Google Analytics, no Facebook pixel. Ever.

    🕊️ Your Control & Consent Choices

    Because we value simplicity, we do not use pop-ups that force you into a labyrinth of toggles. You are always in control. You can:

    • ☑️ Disable all cookies via your browser settings (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari etc.) — the core almond recipes, articles and contact features will still work, except maybe some session-based preferences.
    • ☑️ Delete existing cookies at any time from your browser privacy settings.
    • ☑️ Withdraw implied consent – continuing to browse our site implies acceptance of strictly necessary cookies only. If you prefer not to have even the session cookie, adjust browser settings. We respect Do Not Track signals if enabled.

    ⚙️ Third-Party & External Integrations – Zero Spying

    We do not integrate any external scripts that set cookies. This means: no Google Fonts tracking, no embedded YouTube cookies (we use no video embeds), no external comment systems. Our culinary sanctuary uses only local resources and static content. Any outbound links to social media or recipe platforms open in a new tab without sending referrer information that could be used for tracking. We don't use CDNs that fingerprint your browser.

    🔒 Third-party cookie warning: If you click external links (for example, our official Instagram or GitHub), those sites may use their own cookies. We strongly recommend reviewing their privacy policies separately. Aether Almond Atelier is not responsible for external cookie practices.

    📅 Cookie Duration & Retention Explanation

    As per the table above, our cookies live either for a single session (until you close your browser) or short durations (1 hour to 30 days). The almond_pref_theme cookie lasts 30 days, only if you voluntarily set a persistent preference. The cookie_consent_saved is stored for up to 12 months to honor your choice. All cookie data is stored locally on your device, never transmitted to any external advertising or analytics platform.

    You can manually delete any cookie using your browser’s “Clear browsing data” tool. We encourage transparency – our entire cookie inventory fits on a napkin.

    🌍 Legal Grounding: GDPR & CCPA / CPRA

    GDPR (Art. 5, 6 & ePrivacy Directive): Our use of strictly necessary cookies does not require explicit consent under GDPR, as they are essential for providing the service you request (e.g., session integrity, security). For functional preference cookies (almond_pref_theme), we rely on your affirmative action (clicking “remember my choice”). You can withdraw any time by clearing cookies. We never use non-essential cookies that require opt-in consent.

    CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act): Since we do not sell personal information or use cross-context behavioral advertising cookies, there is no “sale” of data. California residents have the right to opt-out of any future hypothetical tracking – we promise to update this policy if any change occurs.

    🍒 How to Adjust Cookie Settings in Your Browser

    Most modern browsers allow you to manage cookies through their settings/preferences. Here are links to popular browser guides (opened externally):

    • Google Chrome – Block or delete cookies via Settings → Privacy & Security.
    • Firefox – Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.
    • Safari – Preferences → Privacy → Manage website data.
    • Microsoft Edge – Settings → Cookies and site permissions.

    Adjusting these settings may impact the use of session-based preference but will not break core reading experience.
